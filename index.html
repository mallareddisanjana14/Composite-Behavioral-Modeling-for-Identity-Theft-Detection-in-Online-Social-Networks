Composite Behavioral Modeling for Identity Theft Detection in Online Social Networks

About

Projects!

Identity theft in online social networks (OSNs) has emerged as a significant cybersecurity threat due to the exponential growth of digital interactions. This paper presents a compositive behavioral modeling approach for identity theft detection in OSNs by leveraging machine learning techniques. The system comprises two primary modules: Admin and User. The Admin module enables dataset management, preprocessing, model execution (SVM, Random Forest, Decision Tree), and performance evaluation. The User module allows behavior visualization and theft detection. Our proposed system enhances accuracy by integrating multiple classifiers, providing a robust detection mechanism against identity theft.
